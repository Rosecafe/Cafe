Halawet el Jibn

Halawet el Jibn is a traditional cheesey dessert which is still a matter of dispute whether its orginally from Homs or Hama.

We lebanese don't care about the origin and we adopted it into our culture as well, and absolutely love to consume it specifically during Ramadam and Eid and certain celebrations

Visually it looks like a soft pillow consisting of a cheesy dough encompassing a creamy mascorpone filling, garnished with pistachio and dipped in rosey sugar syrup before being devoured

Not only is it fun to make with a friend, you can also skip the gym for the day because of the bicep workout you'll manage to fit in while stirring the pot

So please try this reciepe out!

Ingredients

For the creamy filling

  • macropone
  • a can of honey gymar

For the sugar syrup

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon orange blossom water

For the semolina-cheese dough:

  • 350 grams /12 oz mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup fine semolina
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons of shelled pistachios for garnish

Directions

To make the ashta:

  1. Mix the gymar and Ashta
  2. Chill in the fridge until needed

To make the sugar syrup:

  1. Place the sugar, water, and lemon juice in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir the mixture with a wooden spoon to dissolve the sugar.
  3. Once it boils reduce heat to low and simmer for 7 minutes.
  4. Do not cover the saucepan.
  5. Add the orange blossom water and simmer for 1 more minute.
  6. Set aside to completely cool down.

To make the semolina-cheeese dough:

  1. Add 3/4 cup sugar, 1 cup of fine semolina/ farina, crushed mastic and 1 1/4 cups water to a medium sauce pan and whisk them together over medium-high heat, whisking constantly until the mixture totally thickens.
  2. Reduce heat to low, add the shredded mozzarella cheese stir vigorously with a wooden spoon or spatula until the cheese is incorporated (whisk constantly so you end up with a uniform dough), this happens so fast,
  3. Remove from heat and keep it aside just until the dough is cool enough to handle.
  4. Divide it to two equal balls.

The Assembly

  1. Work with one of the halves!
  2. Place two equal-sized sheets of parchment paper underneath and above the dough, making a kind of dough sandwich.
  3. Then roll out with your rolling pin, keeping the dough sandwiched between the two parchment sheets.
  4. Roll the dough as evenly as you can to create a rectangle shape and about 1 1/4 cm/ 1/2 inch thick, avoid thinning it or the dough will poke.
  5. Remove the top piece of parchment (if you have not already done so).
  6. Cut the dough to a uniform rectangle, the dough doesn’t have to be a truly perfect rectangle. Trim the excess dough and set aside
  7. Using a pastry bag with a large coupler, apply a firm pressure and pipe a line of the filling close to the edge of the dough lengthwise.
  8. Starting at the end closest to you, carefully lift up the edge of the parchment to bring the innermost edge of the semolina-cheese dough up over the filling, using the parchment to encourage the roll to take on a circular shape until the filling(ashta) is totally sealed and the seam sides down.
  9. Then delicately, with a pizza cutter or sharp knife slice off the log from the rest of the dough.
  10. Pipe the filling in the same manner with the remaining part of the semolina-cheese dough, repeat with the second dough.
  11. Chill in the freezer for half an hour or in the fridge overnight
  12. Once chilled, use a sharp knife cut into equal mini morsels about 4-5 cm/1.5 inches long.
  13. Transfer to a pretty platter, garnish each morsel with crushed pistachios, and if you are lucky enough to have jam rose petals, add them for a more attracting garnish
  14. Serve with sugar syrup on the side.
  15. ENJOY! SAHTEEN!
